## My start
To be clear, I was not one of the dudes who want to study outside, I'd rather prefer studying in my cozy bedroom with my desktop and laptop avaliable.

But I have my friend Neng who is always struggling to find a place to study outside, and honestly I think this is a great opportunity to strethen my skills in webdev and solve some problems, and that is how wattogo is born.

In this article, I'm going to track all of my thoughts when building the project and maybe revise along the way.

## First steps
For a project like this, the hardest part was always to choose my tech stack, as I am quite familiar with typescript, I'll just stick with it.

For the map, I choose [OpenStreeetMap's](www.openstreetmap.org) [CARTO](https://carto.com/) version, as it is free to use and good looking.

Then I used supabase as it is well known for its free tier, I think this is sufficient for now, but the original OSM pop up icon looks kinda plain, so I chose Google Material Icons for better look.

## Marker realization
